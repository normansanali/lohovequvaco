Mickie James Wins Big At Native American Music Awards: Talks Music &amp; WWE Evolution

Publish date: 2024-09-05

RAW Superstar Mickie James was the host of this year’s 18th Annual Native American Music Awards; but, she also took home a significant award.

James’ “Left Right Left” was victorious in the 2018 Best Single Recording category:

WWE chief brand officer Stephanie McMahon tweeted the following congratulations:

Congratulations @MickieJames on “Left Right Left” winning Best Single Recording at last night’s @NativeAwards! Well deserved! #nama18

— Stephanie McMahon (@StephMcMahon) October 13, 2018

Fellow RAW Superstar Nia Jax also tweeted the following:

CONGRATULATIONS ????to @MickieJames for winning Best single recording @NativeAwards tonight!!! You should be so proud! You hardworking bad Azz woman! ?? #NativeAwards

— Lina Fanene (@LinaFanene) October 13, 2018

James also performed at the event:

https://twitter.com/dolphspaige/status/1050945540089925632

Fox 11 Los Angeles recently interviewed James and the multi-talented performer opened up about her music career,

“I’ve always loved music. I’ve worked on music and written music; but, it wasn’t until i was actually on the road full time with WWE that I put my first album out. I did that myself. I’m working on my third album now and we’ve put out like five singles. I have a few more coming out. I’m working on a song with Lita Ford, which is amazing because she’s a legend and I have another song that I personally wrote coming out later in the year. I just like putting stuff out there. It’s a different outlet for me because wrestling is so agro and so intense that music allows me to have a different side and a release.”

James also commented on the upcoming WWE Evolution pay-per-view:

“It’s ground breaking for us. I think, because especially in this last year there have been so many monumental moments for women in our industry and it’s really driven by our fans, that they’ve wanted this and now it’s just kind of come to fruition from the first ever Elimination Chamber to the first ever Women’s Royal Rumble match and now we have our very own first ever pay-per-view Evolution. I just hope that this is the beginning of an annual thing because there’s so much talent out there for the females – not just what you see on the mainstream right now on RAW and SmackDown; but, in NXT. Then there’s untapped talent that we haven’t even like – not discovered – we’ve seen them, we know them; but, you guys haven’t seen them yet. There’s just a plethora and with the rise of women’s wrestling and the desire to want that out there from the fans, it’s just incredible.”

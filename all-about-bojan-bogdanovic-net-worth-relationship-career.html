How did Bojan Bogdanović start his Professional Career?

Bojan Bogdanovic began his professional career in Bosnia before moving to the Adriatic League in 2008. He was named an Adriatic League All-Star in only his second season, averaging more than 16 points per game.

Bojan became the first Croatian player to be taken in the NBA draft in 2011 when the Miami Heat chose him as a result of his performance in the Adriatic League.

He was later traded to the Brooklyn Nets, where he played the next four seasons and grew into an important component of the starting lineup. He achieved a career-best with 24 points in a game against the Los Angeles Clippers in 2014.

Bojan moved to Turkey in 2016 to play in the EuroLeague with Fenerbahce. He was named the competition’s MVP after playing a vital role in leading Fenerbahce to the 2017 EuroLeague Championship.

Following his success in the EuroLeague, Bojan returned to the NBA in 2018, signing a two-year contract with the Indiana Pacers. He was traded to the Utah Jazz in 2019 before joining the Detroit Pistons in 2022.

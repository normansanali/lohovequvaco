Snoop Dogg - Bio, Age, Net Worth, Height, Married, Facts

Publish date: 2024-09-24

Table of Biography

Snoop Dogg is a rapper, songwriter, media personality, actor, and entrepreneur from the United States. His career began in 1992 when Snoop Dogg appeared on Doctor Dre’s debut solo single, “Deep Cover,” and then on The Chronic, Dre’s debut solo album. He has sold over 23 million albums in America and 35 million internationally since his beginning.

Early life

Calvin Cordozar Broadus Jr. best known as Snoop Dogg was born on October 20, 1971, in Long Beach, California, the United States of America. As of now, he is 52 years old and holds citizenship in America. Moreover, his born sign is Libra, and is Christian by his religion.

Again, he belongs to the Afro-American ethnicity. He was the son of Vernall Varnado (father) and Beverly Broadus (mother). His father was a Vietnam veteran, singer, and mail carrier. Sadly his father left the family soon after Snoop’s birth. Furthermore, he was named after his stepfather, Calvin Cordozar Broadus Sr. From his childhood, he was interested in singing, rapping, and playing the piano.

Talking about his academic qualification, he completed his high school education at the Long Beach Polytechnic High School in 1989.

Snoop Dogg – Professional career

Snoop Doggy Dogg was his stage name at the beginning. And he began working with Dr. Dre on Dre’s debut solo album, ‘The Chronic,’ which was popular among the fans. In 1993, he released his debut album, ‘Doggystyle,’ which became a huge hit. His second album, ‘Tha Doggfather,’ was released in 1996, and while it received great reviews, it was not as successful as his first.

He signed with Master P’s No Limit Records in 1998 and made his debut with the single “Da Game Is to Be Sold, Not to Be Told”. Moreover, he released other albums, ‘No Limit Top Dogg’ (1999) and ‘Tha Last Meal’ (2000) in the following years. He released his album “Paid Tha Cost to Be da Bo$$” in 2002, which included the hit singles “From tha Church to da Palace” and “Beautiful”.

His album R&G (Rhythm & Gangsta): The Masterpiece, which included the single “Drop It Like It’s Hot,” was published in 2004. He released another album, ‘The Hard Way’ featuring the single “Groupie Luv” in the same year. Critics praised his 2006 album ‘Tha Blue Carpet Treatment,’ as well as its second song “That’s That Shit”.

In the following years, he released several albums, including ‘Ego Trippin’ (2008), ‘Malice n Wonderland’ (2009), and ‘Doggumentary’ (2010). Furthermore, he changed his stage name to Snoop Lion in 2012. His documentary ‘Reincarnated’ was released in March 2013, followed by his new studio album of the same name the following month. ‘Coolaid’ (2016), ‘Neva Left’ (2017), ‘Bible of Love’ (2018), and ‘I Wanna Thank Me’ are among his other albums (2019).

Guest appearances in movies and television shows

In films such as ‘The Wash’ (2001), ‘Bones’ (2001), and ‘Mac and Devin Go to High School'(2012), he has appeared as a guest. He also appeared in several films as a supporting or cameo actor, including ‘Half Baked’ (1998), ‘Training Day’ (2001), ‘Starsky & Hutch’ (2004), and ‘Brüno’ (2009). He also appeared on TV shows like ‘Doggy Fizzle Televizzle,’ a sketch comedy show, and ‘Snoop Dogg’s Father Hood,’ a reality show.

Awards

The rapper has won several awards and recognitions. He won three Billboard Music Awards in 1994 ‘Top Billboard 200 Album Artist – Male,’ ‘Top R&B Album Artist,’ and ‘Top R&B Album Artist – Male’. For the song ‘Doggy Dogg World,’ he won the MTV Video Music Awards in the category of ‘Best Rap Video’ in 1994. In 1995, he won the American Music Award for “Favorite Rap/Hip-Hop Artist” for his music.

Snoop Dogg – Net Worth 2024

For his song ‘Drop It Like It’s Hot,’ he received the ‘International Dance Music Award’ in the ‘Best Rap/Hip-Hop Dance Track’ category in 2005. Being a multi-talented person, he has earned a huge sum of money in his career.

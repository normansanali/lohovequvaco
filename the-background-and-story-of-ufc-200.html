The Background and Story of UFC 200

Publish date: 2024-07-29

The Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC) 200 was a landmark event in the history of mixed martial arts (MMA).

It was the 200th event produced by the UFC and took place on July 9, 2016, at the T-Mobile Arena in Las Vegas, Nevada.

UFC 200 was a significant milestone for the organization, featuring a star-studded lineup and marking a momentous occasion in the evolution of the sport.

Inception of UFC 200

The UFC, founded in 1993, was initially created to determine the most effective martial art in a real fight between competitors of different fighting disciplines.

Over the years, it evolved and grew into a global phenomenon, becoming the premier organization in MMA. UFC 200 was a culmination of this journey, celebrating the sport's growth and popularity.

Significance within the UFC Franchise

UFC 200 was a highly anticipated event due to its numerical significance and the caliber of fights scheduled. It was designed to be a grand spectacle, with an emphasis on marquee matchups and high-profile fighters.

The event was intended to showcase the best of what the UFC had to offer, drawing attention to the organization's top talent and providing fans with memorable fights.

Key Figures Associated with UFC 200

Brock Lesnar

One of the key figures associated with UFC 200 was Brock Lesnar, a former UFC Heavyweight Champion and WWE superstar.

Lesnar's return to the octagon at UFC 200 generated substantial excitement among fans, as he possessed a larger-than-life persona and a background in collegiate wrestling and professional wrestling.

Jon Jones

Another pivotal figure tied to UFC 200 was Jon Jones, a dominant force in the UFC's Light Heavyweight division. But Jones was initially slated to fight Daniel Cormier in the main event but was removed from the card due to a potential doping violation, creating significant upheaval in the event's lineup.

Amanda Nunes

Amanda Nunes, the reigning UFC Women's Bantamweight Champion, also played a crucial role in UFC 200.

Nunes headlined the event and faced Miesha Tate in a highly anticipated title fight, solidifying her status as a top female fighter in the sport.

Notable Controversies and Milestones

Jon Jones' Removal

The removal of Jon Jones from the main event due to a doping violation was a major controversy leading up to UFC 200.

Jones' absence significantly impacted the promotional aspect of the event, causing a last-minute rearrangement of the fight card.

Brock Lesnar's Return

Brock Lesnar's return to the UFC after a four-year hiatus was a milestone moment for UFC 200.

His crossover appeal from the world of professional wrestling brought a new level of mainstream attention to the event, attracting fans from both MMA and professional wrestling communities.

The Legacy of UFC 200

UFC 200 left a lasting legacy within the MMA community. Despite the controversies surrounding the event, it remains a significant chapter in the UFC's history.

The event showcased the organization's ability to draw a global audience and highlighted the star power of its athletes. Additionally, UFC 200 demonstrated the UFC's commitment to delivering high-quality entertainment and pushing the boundaries of combat sports.

UFC 200 stands as a pivotal moment in the history of the UFC. The event represented a culmination of the organization's growth and success, featuring notable figures, compelling matchups, and both controversies and milestones.

Career and Professional Life

Victoria began film acting appearing in two independent short films in 2014. She came into the limelight after her portrayal of Eleanor “Nell” Crain Vance in the Netflix horror series ‘The Haunting of Hill House’ in 2018. Moreover, her performance was praised by critics, with Netflix stating

“If this is the depth of talent Victoria Pedretti has shown us in merely three years, just imagine what the future holds.”

Likewise, Victoria also earned Saturn Award and MTV Award nominations in 2019 for the role. She auditioned for the role of Guinevere Beck on the Netflix thriller series ‘You’ in 2017. Though she was initially rejected, the directors later cast her as Love Quinn in the show’s second season. Therefore, after seeing her previous acting work and her chemistry with her co-star ‘Penn Badgley’.

Likewise, the second season was released on December 26, 2019, with Victoria’s performance and the show being received positively. Moreover, she will reprise her role for the third season, scheduled for release sometime in 2021. Victoria appeared as ‘Leslie Van Houten’ in Quentin Tarantino’s comedy-drama film ‘Once Upon a Time in Hollywood’ in 2019, which was a commercial success.

More about her career

She guest-starred in the anthology series ‘Amazing Stories’ as Evelyn Porter in 2020, and she also starred as Katherine in the biographical drama film ‘Shirley’, the latter of which received positive reviews from critics. The same year, she starred as Danielle “Dani” Clayton on the Netflix series ‘The Haunting of Bly Manor’, the second season of the anthology series ‘The Haunting of Hill House’.

Moreover, the series and Pedretti’s performance received critical acclaim, with The Hollywood Reporter writing that her performance “generates instant empathy”. And, the role earned her a nomination from the Critics’ Choice Super Awards and helped establish her as a modern-day ‘scream queen’.

Additionally, Victoria is a trained singer and can sing in the Soprano/Belter range, the highest range for female voices. Meanwhile, at University, she had given a vocal performance in the Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra’s theatre production titled ‘A Musical Valentine: The Songs of Marvin Hamlisch’.
